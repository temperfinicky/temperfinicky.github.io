Te Araroa

Te Araroa (The Long Pathway) is New Zealand's long distance tramping route, stretching roughly 3000 km along the length of the country's two main islands from Cape Reinga to Bluff. It is made up of a mixture of older tracks and walkways, new tracks and link sections alongside roads. Tramping the full length of the trail generally takes three to six months ↳ Wikipedia.

Between October 2018 and March 2019 we will be attempting a south-bound through-walk. Below we will gradually share some of our best (and worst?) experiences along the trail. For more frequent updates you can also find us on Twitter ↳ @temperfinicky. See ya!

Lofoten

Lofoten is an archipelago and a traditional district in the county of Nordland, Norway. It is known for a distinctive scenery with dramatic mountains and peaks, open sea and sheltered bays, beaches and untouched lands. Though lying within the Arctic Circle, the archipelago experiences one of the world's largest elevated temperature anomalies relative to its high latitude ↳ Wikipedia.

In early June 2018 we attempted a 10-day south-bound trek — losely following the route described ↳ here.

After a historically snowy winter our travel window proved to be a tad bit early. It was the pronounced rainfalls and thunderstorms almost throughout our visit, however, that forced us to deviate from our planned route twice (once due to snowfields and a developing storm at 440m elevation north of Svolvær, and another time due to a knee-deep flooded plains east of Leknes).

While the weather conditions did bring us to our mental limits occasionally (hey, as long as the sleeping bag stays dry! Right?), they only served to further attenuate the harsh, ragged beauty of the Lofoten landscape. A beauty one needs to experience with all of ones senses, and which photopraphs only inadequately capture. We’ll certainly be back!

On an unrelated closing note: in case you ever thought about signing up for the ↳ Arctic Triple ultra trail (because, you know, other 100 mile runs are for #?%!), don’t do it. Just don’t. Please?
